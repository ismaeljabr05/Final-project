A total solar eclipse

occurs when the moon passes directly in front of the sun, completely blocking it from view on Earth, causing total darkness in specific areas. This rare phenomenon happens only in certain regions, where complete darkness can be witnessed for a few minutes.

The aurora borealis

is a phenomenon that occurs when charged particles from solar winds interact with Earth's magnetic field, resulting in colorful lights appearing in the sky. This phenomenon primarily occurs in polar regions, but it can rarely be seen in areas far from the poles.
